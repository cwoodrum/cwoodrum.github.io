About Me

I'm currently a graduate student and NSF Graduate Research Fellow at Steward Observatory, University of Arizona working with Professor Marcia Rieke and the James Webb Space Telescope Near-IR Camera (NIRCam) team. I recently graduated with my B.S. in Physics from the University of Oregon where I did research in galaxy evolution.

Research

My research uses the universe as a time machine to study the evolution of galaxies over cosmic time. See below for more information on some projects I have worked on.

Figure: HST/ACS images of our sample of passive bulge-dominated field galaxies. We compared these field galaxies to cluster galaxies at similar redshifts. Our results may indicate that the environment causes differences in the stellar populations of bulge-dominated galaxies. See my ADS link above to read my first author refereed publication.

Photo: I worked as a NASA intern with the Fermi Gamma-ray Space Telescope Gamma-ray Burst Monitor team. I aided in the search for the electromagnetic counterpart to gravitational wave events by optimizing the search algorithm that combs through Fermi data looking for gamma-ray bursts. Photo credit: NASA/General Dynamics (artist's impression).

Broader Impacts

My path to astrophysics was nontraditional. I am a first generation high school graduate from a small, impoverished town. I now recognize how beneficial it would have been for me to have met someone who worked in a STEM career earlier in life. For this reason, I am very passionate about public outreach and am interested in assisting those who have faced barriers to education, especially marginalized groups. Please contact me if you would like to collaborate on educational projects.

Photo: I operated a telescope for open public nights at Pine Mountain Observatory where I gave tours of the night sky for 4 years. Pine Mountain is where I grew both my passion and skills for public outreach.

Photo: I was a keynote speaker for Churchill High School's Women in Science and Engineering Symposium. I love sharing my research with curious public audiences.
